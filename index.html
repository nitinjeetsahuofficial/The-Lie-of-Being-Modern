What this book asks

Are we truly modern,
or merely distracted?

We live in an age of smartphones and skyscrapers — yet anxiety, emptiness, and confusion have never been higher. This book dares to ask: what if modernity itself is the greatest lie we have ever been sold?

01

The Illusion

Technology advances, but wisdom retreats. We mistake innovation for evolution — and confuse connectivity with meaning.

02

The Warning

Every great civilization believed itself to be the apex of human achievement. Every one of them was wrong. Are we any different?

03

The Truth

Beneath the noise of modernity lies a timeless framework for living — one that no algorithm, trend, or ideology can replace.

Central Premise

"We inherited the tools of modernity — but somewhere along the way, we forgot to ask if we were using them to build something worth living for."

— Nitinjeet Sahu

Nitinjeet

Indian Author · Poet · World Record Holder

Nitinjeet Sahu is a young Indian author and poet from Odisha who writes from raw experience rather than polished theory. He is the author of Living Heritage: Poems on Wisdom, Culture and Nature and The Last Exam: Exploring the Inner Soul of a UPSC Aspirant — the book that earned him a World Record as one of the youngest authors in India to write a motivational book on UPSC. In The Lie of Being Modern, he turns his lens on civilisation itself.
